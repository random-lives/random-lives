# Biggest Events in Human History by Number of People Personally Affected

**Definition of "personally affected"**: Your daily life was materially disrupted — food supply, work, movement, safety, family structure. Not just "you heard about it."

**Reference**: ~66 billion humans have ever lived (our project's median estimate; see "How many people have ever lived?" blog post). The commonly cited ~117 billion from PRB is likely too high.

| Rank | Event | Year(s) | World Pop | Est. Affected | % World Pop | % All Humans (~66B) | Brief Reasoning |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **COVID-19 pandemic** | 2020-2023 | 7.9B | 7.5-7.8B | ~97% | ~11.7% | Lockdowns, school closures, economic disruption, travel bans hit virtually every country. Exclude only the most isolated/subsistence populations. |
| 2 | **World War II** | 1939-1945 | 2.3B | 1.7-2.0B | ~80% | ~2.8% | Direct combatants + occupied territories + colonial subjects. China (invasion/famine), USSR (invasion), British Empire inc. India (Bengal Famine, 2.5M troops), USA (rationing/conscription), France+colonies, Australia, etc. Uninvolved: most of South America, Spain/Portugal (neutral). |
| 3 | **2008 Global Financial Crisis** | 2008-2010 | 6.7B | 3.5-4.5B | ~55-65% | ~6.1% | GDP fell in half the world's countries. Severe in US, Europe, Japan, Russia. But subsistence farmers in rural Africa/Asia less directly affected. Debatable whether this meets "material disruption" threshold for most. |
| 4 | **1918 Influenza Pandemic** | 1918-1920 | 1.8B | 1.0-1.5B | ~65-80% | ~1.9% | 500M infected (1/3 of world). 40-100M dead. Devastated India (16.7M dead), US, Europe. But no coordinated lockdowns; many rural populations less affected. |
| 5 | **Great Depression** | 1929-1939 | 2.0B | 0.8-1.2B | ~45-55% | ~1.5% | Severe in industrialized/trade-connected economies. World trade fell 50-65%. But most of world population was in subsistence agriculture (rural China, India, Africa). |
| 6 | **Indian Demonetization** | 2016 | 7.4B | ~1.0-1.2B | ~15% | ~1.7% | 86% of India's currency invalidated overnight. Cash used for 95%+ of transactions. Virtually all 1.3B Indians affected. |
| 7 | **Chinese Great Leap Forward / Great Famine** | 1958-1962 | 3.0B | ~650-700M | ~22% | ~1.0% | China's entire ~650M population affected: forced collectivization, communal dining, then catastrophic famine (15-55M dead). |
| 8 | **World War I** | 1914-1918 | 1.8B | ~550-750M | ~35-40% | ~1.0% | More geographically constrained than WWII. Core: France, Germany, UK, Russia, Austria-Hungary, Ottoman Empire, Italy, Belgium, Serbia. Colonial disruption more limited than WWII. Spain/Portugal uninvolved. |
| 9 | **Fall of the Soviet Union** | 1989-1992 | 5.3B | ~400-500M | ~8-9% | ~0.7% | USSR (~290M) + Eastern Bloc (~135M): economic collapse, hyperinflation, supply chain breakdown. Russian male life expectancy dropped from 64 to 57. |
| 10 | **Chinese Communist Revolution / Civil War** | 1945-1952 | 2.5B | ~500-550M | ~21% | ~0.8% | China's ~540M population. Civil war + revolutionary transformation (land reform, class struggle, collectivization) affected virtually everyone. |
| 11 | **Mongol Conquests** | 1206-1300 | ~400M | ~100-200M | ~35-45% | ~0.23% | Conquered China, Central Asia, Persia, Iraq, Russia. Death toll 20-60M. Spread across a century. |
| 12 | **Black Death** | 1346-1353 | ~440M | ~150-200M | ~40-45% | ~0.27% | Killed 30-60% of Europe (~75M affected, 25-50M died). Also devastated Middle East and North Africa. East/South Asia, sub-Saharan Africa largely unaffected. |
| 13 | **Napoleonic Wars** | 1803-1815 | ~1.0B | ~100-200M | ~15% | ~0.23% | Most of Europe dragged in. France (conscription), Spain (Peninsular War), Russia (1812 invasion), Germany/Prussia, Italy, Austria, Poland, Netherlands. 5M European deaths. |
| 14 | **Taiping Rebellion** | 1850-1864 | 1.2B | ~150-250M | ~15-20% | ~0.30% | Ravaged 17 provinces in southern/central China. Death toll 20-70M. Core disruption zone: 150-250M. |
| 15 | **Russian Revolution and Civil War** | 1917-1922 | 1.8B | ~150-170M | ~9% | ~0.24% | Russian Empire's ~170M virtually all affected. 7-12M killed. Industrial production fell to 15% of pre-war levels. |
| 16 | **Partition of India** | 1947 | 2.5B | ~100-390M | ~5-15% | ~0.37% | 15-17M displaced, 1-2M killed, ~50-100M in directly affected border regions. Broader: entire subcontinent (~390M) experienced political upheaval. |
| 17 | **Thirty Years' War** | 1618-1648 | ~550M | ~20-30M | ~4-5% | ~0.04% | Germany's population fell from ~20M to ~12M (40% decline). Concentrated in Holy Roman Empire. |
| 18 | **An Lushan Rebellion** | 755-763 | ~250M | ~50-80M | ~25-30% | ~0.10% | Tang China census dropped from ~53M to ~17M. Northern China bore the brunt. Chang'an and Luoyang sacked. |
| 19 | **Columbian Exchange / European colonization** | 1492-1600 | ~450-500M | ~50-80M | ~12-16% | ~0.10% | Pre-Columbian Americas: 45-60M, 80-95% population decline by 1600. Played out over a century. |
| 20 | **Plague of Justinian** | 541-549 | ~200M | ~40-70M | ~25-30% | ~0.08% | Killed up to 25% of Eastern Mediterranean population. Byzantine Empire, Sasanian Persia, North Africa, Italy. |
| 21 | **Decolonization of Africa** | 1955-1975 | 3-4B | ~150-250M | ~5-7% | ~0.30% | Africa's ~280M in 1960. Some transitions smooth, others violent (Algeria, Congo, Angola). Spread over 20 years. |
| 22 | **Arab Spring** | 2011-2015 | 7.0B | ~100-200M | ~2-3% | ~0.23% | Egypt (~82M), Syria (~21M, civil war), Libya (~6M), Yemen (~25M), Tunisia (~11M). |
| 23 | **Seven Years' War** | 1756-1763 | ~700M | ~50-100M | ~10-14% | ~0.11% | Fought on 5 continents. Prussia devastated. Colonial theaters in North America, India, Caribbean, Philippines. |
| 24 | **Fall of Western Roman Empire** | 376-476 | ~200M | ~30-50M | ~18-25% | ~0.06% | Century-long process. Western Empire ~30-40M experienced invasions, economic collapse, urban depopulation. |
| 25 | **Korean War** | 1950-1953 | 2.5B | ~50-70M | ~2-3% | ~0.09% | Korea's ~30M almost all severely affected. China sent 3M troops. US experienced economic mobilization. |
| 26 | **Vietnam War** | 1955-1975 | 3-4B | ~50-80M | ~1.5-2% | ~0.10% | Vietnam (~35M), Laos (~3M), Cambodia (~7M). US draft/protests but most daily lives not materially disrupted. |
| 27 | **Atlantic Slave Trade** | 1500-1900 | varies | ~25-50M cumul. | varies | ~0.06% | 12-15M transported, ~2M died in transit, plus massive disruption to source communities. Spread over 400 years. |
